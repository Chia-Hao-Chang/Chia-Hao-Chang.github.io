Research

I am always interested in finding concise mathematical models to help people make optimal decisions and to understand the mechanism behind daily-life phenomena. To be more specific, I am particularly fascinated by probability theory, game theory. My research interests largely fall into (but are not limited to) the following topics:

Probability and Stochastic Processes

  • Stochastic Modeling and Stochastic Systems

  • Stochastic Optimal Control and Dynamic Programming

Mathematical Economics

  • Game Theory

    • Strategic Experimentation, Learning and Information Aggregation in Games

  • Decision Theory

    • Decision Theory, Choice Theory, Subjective Probability, Ambiguity

Research Experiences

  • Strategic Queueing Systems (with Prof. John Hasenbein)

    • Investigated the parameter sensitivity in a complex MDP model.

    • Proposed a novel method by approximation

    • Presented at 2019 INFORMS Annual Conference, Seattle, WA.

  • Game Theory (with Prof. Thomas Wiseman)

    • Extended a staged financing model by introducing a private signal.

    • Compared the associated perfect Bayesian equilibria under different information structures.
